ADAPTATION RECOMMENDATIONS
HIGH Add defrost-zone scheduling guard
When outdoor temp is −4°C to +7°C with RH >70%, the Ecodan is already stress-running defrost cycles every 30–90 min. Avoid model-triggered on/off commands during this window — each forced restart prolongs the defrost queue. Suggested fix: add a 15-min post-defrost lockout (read FTC controller defrost flag before issuing next command).
MEDIUM Reduce night setback: 15°C → 16.5°C target
Current 3.2°C night drop forces the slab to re-absorb full heat from 15°C each morning — est. 0.8 kWh/day wasted reheating thermal mass. Raising night target to 16.5°C keeps slab warm overnight. The HP runs briefer top-ups at night rather than a cold morning ramp. Net saving: ~€18/month at current rates.
LOW Enforce 6-min minimum off-time between cycles
3 short-cycling events detected this month (off-time <6 min). While the Ecodan has a built-in compressor protection timer, software-level enforcement adds a safety layer and reduces refrigerant pressure imbalance events. Add a min_off_minutes = 6 lockout after any HP stop signal.

💡 When does the minimum bite? Binding cycles share a pattern: outdoor temp > 8°C, low wind, short DP dip. The spread recovers within ~12 min of heating start — the system would prefer a 12–15 min burst, but the floor extends it to 20 min. These conditions occur mainly on mild days and are inherently low-penalty because the spread re-stabilises quickly regardless.
